Arsenal in the Community: Empowering Youth Through Sports and Education

Established in 1985, Arsenal in the Community leverages the club's influence to positively impact young people's lives. Delivering sports, social, and educational programmes to over 5,000 individuals weekly, the initiative focuses on fostering a sense of belonging and personal development.

Key Programmes:

  • Positive Futures: An estate-based youth engagement programme funded by Islington Council, offering regular activities to support young people aged 8 to 18 within their local communities.

  • Premier League Inspires: A year-round initiative across Islington, Hackney, and Camden, helping young people aged 11–18 build confidence, develop life skills, and improve mental well-being through workshops and mentoring.

The Arsenal Foundation: Global Outreach and Support

The Arsenal Foundation utilizes the power of football to inspire and support young people in North London and worldwide. Through various programmes, the foundation addresses issues such as mental health, youth empowerment, social inclusion, and education.

Notable Initiatives:

  • Coaching for Life: In partnership with Save the Children, this programme has supported over 5,000 children in Jordan's Za'atari Refugee Camp, using football to build resilience, mental health, and life skills.

  • Gunners Fund: A grant-making initiative backing grassroots projects, including bereavement groups, dementia art classes, LGBTQ+ football programmes, and food banks, aiming to make a tangible difference in local communities.

International Collaborations: Enhancing Lives Through Football

Arsenal extends its community efforts globally, collaborating with organizations to improve children's lives through football.​

Highlighted Project:

  • Indonesia Initiative: In collaboration with the UEFA Foundation for Children and Save the Children, The Arsenal Foundation is working to benefit over 1,000 children in Jakarta. The project focuses on renovating seven football pitches and providing safe spaces for children, particularly girls, to play and learn, aiming to reduce child labor and improve access to education.
